Consulting Presentation Templates

Consultants sell expertise, but they're judged on deliverables. A sharp presentation signals sharp thinking. A sloppy one undermines your credibility before you say a word. These templates give you the professional foundation top-tier firms use.

The consulting presentation standard

McKinsey, BCG, Bain — love them or hate them, they set the standard for business presentations. Clean layouts, structured thinking, data that supports narrative. Clients expect it.

You don't need to copy their style exactly. But you need to meet their bar for professionalism, clarity, and structure.

What consultants present

  • Proposals — scope, approach, team, investment
  • Strategy presentations — analysis, recommendations, roadmap
  • Workshop facilitation — collaborative sessions, exercises, synthesis
  • Final deliverables — findings, recommendations, implementation plans
  • Thought leadership — research, insights, industry perspectives
  • Project updates — progress, findings, next steps

Structure = credibility

Consulting presentations need rigorous structure. Situation, complication, resolution. Hypothesis, analysis, recommendation. The frameworks matter as much as the content.

These templates embed those frameworks. You fill in your thinking; the structure is already there.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these templates appropriate for Big 4 / MBB-style work?

Yes. Clean, professional, structured — the aesthetic top-tier firms expect. Customize with your brand, keep the professional foundation.

Do you have templates for strategy presentations?

Yes. The strategy presentation template follows classic consulting structure — situation analysis, strategic options, recommendations, implementation.

How do I structure complex consulting deliverables?

Lead with the recommendation, then support it. Clients want the answer first, then the analysis. Our templates are built for this executive-first structure.

Can I use these for workshop facilitation?

Yes. The workshop template is designed for collaborative sessions — activities, discussion frameworks, synthesis slides.
