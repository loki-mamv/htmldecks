Biotech & Pharma Presentation Templates

Biotech presentations bridge science and business. You need to communicate rigorous research to investors who want returns, partners who want data, and regulators who want documentation. These templates help with all three.

The translation challenge

Scientists speak one language, investors another. The best biotech presentations translate between them — conveying scientific rigor while making the business case clear.

Biotech presentation types

  • Investor pitches — pipeline, mechanism, market, team, capital needs
  • Scientific presentations — data, methodology, publications, conferences
  • Partner discussions — licensing, co-development, deal terms
  • Regulatory presentations — trial design, safety data, submissions
  • Board updates — pipeline progress, financials, strategy
  • R&D updates — internal science reviews, project status

Data presentation matters

Biotech lives and dies by data. Clinical results, preclinical data, mechanism studies — your presentations need to show complex data clearly. Our templates include layouts designed for scientific data visualization.

Multiple audience depths

CSOs want mechanism details. CFOs want burn rate. VCs want market size. Structure presentations with layers — headline for everyone, depth available for those who want it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I present clinical data effectively?

Yes. The templates include layouts for trial data — endpoints, p-values, Kaplan-Meier curves, safety profiles. Clear presentation of complex results.

Do you have templates for investor pitches?

Yes. The startup pitch and fundraising templates work for biotech — pipeline overview, mechanism, market opportunity, team, capital needs.

How do I communicate science to non-scientists?

Lead with the outcome — "This drug could cure X." Then explain how. Mechanism supports the story; it doesn't lead it.

Can I use these for regulatory presentations?

Yes. The board meeting and research presentation templates provide the structure and documentation rigor regulatory contexts require.
