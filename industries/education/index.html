Presentation Templates for Education

Teachers and professors spend too much time fighting with presentation software and not enough time teaching. HTML Decks gives you clean, accessible presentations that work on every student device — Chromebooks, iPads, phones, ancient library computers. One file, every screen.

The Classroom Reality

Education has a unique challenge: your audience has the widest range of devices imaginable. One student has the latest MacBook. Another has a school-issued Chromebook from 2018. A third is watching on their phone. Your presentation needs to look good on all of them.

PowerPoint requires software most students don't have. Google Slides needs reliable internet — good luck with that in half of America's schools. HTML files just work. Any browser, any device, any connection. Even offline once loaded.

Educational Use Cases

  • Lecture presentations — Clean slides that are readable from the back of the hall
  • Online courses — Self-paced modules that work on any learning platform
  • Student assignments — A creative alternative to yet another PowerPoint project
  • Administrative presentations — Board meetings, parent nights, accreditation reviews
  • Research presentations — Conference talks and thesis defenses
  • Training materials — Faculty onboarding and professional development

Frequently Asked Questions

Can students view HTML Decks presentations on Chromebooks?

Yes. Chromebooks run Chrome, which opens HTML files perfectly. This is one of the biggest advantages for K-12 schools where Chromebooks are the standard device.

Can I embed videos in educational presentations?

Yes. HTML supports embedded video from YouTube, Vimeo, or local video files. Great for lecture recordings, demonstrations, or supplementary content.

Are HTML Decks templates good for online courses?

Very. They work on any device without plugins, load fast on slow connections, and can be embedded in any LMS. Perfect for asynchronous learning content.

Can students create their own HTML Decks presentations?

Absolutely. It's a great way to teach basic web literacy while getting creative presentations. Students learn HTML fundamentals while completing assignments.

Do these work with classroom projectors?

Yes. Open the file on the classroom computer, full-screen the browser, and present. Works with every projector and display system we've tested.
