Demo Day Presentation Template

Demo day is the Super Bowl of startup presentations. 3-5 minutes. Dozens of investors. One shot. You're not trying to close funding — you're trying to get the follow-up meeting. This template focuses on that.

5 minutes is not a pitch

It's a hook. You can't explain everything about your company in 5 minutes. Don't try.

Instead, create enough intrigue that investors want the 30-minute conversation. Lead with your strongest proof point, make one thing memorable, and end with a clear ask.

Demo day sequence

  • Opening hook — 10 seconds to grab attention. Not your name
  • The problem — make investors feel the pain
  • The solution — what you built, in one sentence
  • Why now — what market shift makes this possible
  • Traction — your best proof this is real
  • Business model — how you make money. One sentence
  • The ask — what you're raising and why
  • Contact — how to follow up

Designed for the stage

Projectors. Large screens. Viewing from 30+ feet. This template uses large fonts, high contrast, minimal text. Every slide readable from the back row.

You should be able to present it blind

Demo day decks are memorized, not read. The visuals support your story — they don't tell it. Practice until you don't need to look at the screen.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many slides for a 5-minute demo day?

8-10 max. One slide per 30-40 seconds. Every slide must be essential.

Should I do a live demo?

Only if you can pull it off in 30-60 seconds. Live demos are risky — tech fails at the worst time. Consider screenshots or a recorded walkthrough instead.

What's the most important demo day slide?

Traction. If your numbers impress, lead with them. Investors see dozens of pitches — strong metrics are what they remember.

How do I stand out at demo day?

Don't try to be clever. Be clear. Strong hook. One memorable number. Crystal-clear ask. Clarity wins over creativity every time.
