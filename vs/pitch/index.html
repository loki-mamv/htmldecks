HTML Decks vs Pitch

Pitch is a beautifully designed, VC-backed presentation tool built for teams. It has slick collaboration features, nice templates, and that startup polish. But it's also cloud-only, requires accounts, and charges per seat. HTML Decks takes the opposite approach: one file, no accounts, no per-user fees. Here's how they stack up.

Collaboration vs. Simplicity

Pitch is built around team collaboration — multiple people editing, commenting, and iterating on presentations together. If that's your core workflow, Pitch handles it well.

But most professionals don't need everyone editing the same deck simultaneously. They need to create a presentation, then share it with people who just need to view it. For that workflow, Pitch's collaboration features are overhead you're paying for but not using.

HTML Decks is simple: you create the presentation, then share the file. Recipients don't need accounts. They just open it.

The Per-Seat Problem

Pitch charges per user. That's fine for a 3-person startup, but a 20-person team at $30/user/month is $7,200/year for presentation software. That's a lot of money for something you might use a few times a month.

HTML Decks is $29 total. Share it with your whole team. No per-seat fees, no usage limits, no annual renewal.

When to Choose HTML Decks

  • You want to avoid per-seat pricing
  • You need presentations that work offline
  • You share presentations with people who shouldn't need accounts
  • You prefer owning files over cloud platforms
  • You create presentations individually, then share

When to Choose Pitch

  • Your team frequently collaborates on presentations in real-time
  • You need presentation analytics and viewer tracking
  • You value the specific Pitch templates and design tools
  • Per-seat pricing fits your budget

The Bottom Line

Pitch is a good product aimed at teams that need constant collaboration. Most professionals don't. If you create presentations and then share them — rather than editing together in real-time — HTML Decks is simpler, cheaper, and more portable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Pitch better for teams than HTML Decks?

For real-time collaboration, yes. Pitch is designed for teams editing together. But most teams have one person create the deck, then share it. For that workflow, HTML Decks is simpler.

Does Pitch's analytics feature matter?

Pitch can tell you who viewed your presentation and when. That's useful for sales follow-ups. If view tracking is essential to your workflow, it's a genuine advantage.

How does Pitch's pricing compare to HTML Decks?

Pitch Pro is $30/user/month. A 5-person team pays $1,800/year. HTML Decks is $29 one-time for everyone. Do the math.

Can I share HTML Decks with people who use Pitch?

Yes. HTML files open in any browser. Your Pitch-using colleagues can view HTML Decks presentations without installing anything or having accounts.
