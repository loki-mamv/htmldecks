The Authenticity Question

When you present an AI-generated deck, you're presenting someone else's words. For internal or casual use, that might be fine. But for pitches, sales, or leadership presentations, authenticity matters. Investors want to hear your thinking, not ChatGPT's approximation of it.

When to Choose HTML Decks

  • You want your presentations to reflect your actual thinking
  • You need consistency with your brand voice and style
  • You present to audiences who would detect AI-generated content
  • You want to own your files without platform dependency
  • You prefer templates you control over AI you hope gets it right

When to Choose Gamma

  • You need quick internal presentations with minimal effort
  • The content is routine and doesn't require originality
  • You're brainstorming and want AI to help structure ideas
  • Time is more important than authenticity

The Bottom Line

Gamma is an impressive technology demo. For high-stakes presentations, it's a shortcut that can backfire. HTML Decks gives you the structure and design; you bring the substance. That's a more reliable formula for presentations that matter.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Gamma create a good pitch deck automatically?

It can create a pitch deck structure with placeholder content. But the actual pitch — your story, your metrics, your vision — still needs to come from you. At that point, you might as well start with a template.

Is AI-generated content detectable?

Experienced readers often sense something is off — generic phrasing, surface-level insights, lack of specific detail. For important presentations, that impression hurts you.

What's Gamma's advantage over templates?

Speed. If you need something fast and don't care about depth, AI generation is faster than filling in templates. But for presentations worth doing well, the time investment in real content pays off.

Can I use Gamma to start and then customize?

Yes, but heavy customization often takes as long as starting from a template. And you're still dependent on Gamma's platform for the base file.
